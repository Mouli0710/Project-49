Interesting Facts About Camel

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can’t eat.
  • Camels can completely shut their nostrils during sandstorms.

Interesting Facts About Chameleon

  • Chameleons have eyes in the backs of their heads.
  • Chameleons come in a lot of different shapes and sizes.
  • Chameleons are tree-huggers.

Interesting Facts About Pigeon

  • Pigeons are incredibly complex and intelligent animals.
  • Pigeons are renowned for their outstanding navigational abilities.
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities.

Interesting Facts About Teddy Bear

  • The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
  • Washable teddy bears didn't appear until 1954 when the stuffing was switched to an easier to clean nylon fill.
  • The official term for a teddy bear collector is an ‘arctophile’.

Interesting Facts About Panda

  • These magnificent mammals are omnivores.
  • Giant pandas grow to between 1.2m and 1.5m, and weigh between 75kg and 135kg.
  • Baby pandas are born pink and measure about 15cm.

Interesting Facts About Cicada

  • Cicadas are oval-shaped, winged insects that provide a buzzing and clicking song heard in nature throughout the summer.
  • There are around 3,000 cicada species.
  • Cicadas can be black, brown or green and can have red, white or blue eyes.
